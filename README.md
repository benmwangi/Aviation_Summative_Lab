# Aviation Accident Analysis
Analysis of flight accidents from 1983 in United States of America for a plane insurer. The research findings were that overall destcrution and injury rates were low. Smaller planes were more prone to destruction and passengers facing serious or fatal injuries than the larger planes. Other factors such as weather condition and purpose were analysed. Adverse weather conditions seemed to have moderate impact on injury and destruction rates and was more impactful on smaller plane makes. The purpose of the flight represented low impact on destruction and injury rates apart from test flights which had a close relationship with injury and destruction rates.

Suggestions for the client would be to encourage smaller plane pilots to undertake IFR training for better preparedness for flying in adverse weather. Require the use of Certified Flight instructors, thorough underwriting, risk data from previous makes and expert endorsements to aid in mitigating accidents.
